Who’s Hot & Who’s Not

Jonathan Taylor

RB | Colts

Week 1-3 Avg: 57.0 rushing yards / 0.0 total touchdowns

Week 4-11 Avg: 118.9 rushing yards / 1.9 total touchdowns

Jonathan Taylor burst onto the scene in 2020 as a rookie, with the third most rushing yards in the NFL behind only Derrick Henry and Dalvin Cook. After a slow start to the year, Taylor has turned into a one-man wrecking crew, highlighted by his 5 total touchdown performance in Week 11 against a tough Bills defense. No running back has won the NFL MVP award since Adrian Peterson in 2012, but if Taylor keeps playing like this, he might force himself into the conversation.

Keenan Allen

WR | Chargers

Week 1-8 Avg: 6.4 receptions / 70.9 receiving yards

Week 9-11 Avg: 9.7 receptions / 104.7 receiving yards

Keenan Allen enjoyed some early season success in 2021, but seemed to be lacking in the big games that we have come to expect from the receiver who averaged 101 catches for 1,195 yards from 2017-2020. However, over his last three games, quarterback Justin Herbert has clearly focused on getting the ball into Allen’s hands, and it would not surprise us to see this success continue for the rest of the season.

DJ Moore

WR | Panthers

Week 1-4 Avg: 7.5 receptions / 99.5 receiving yards

Week 5-11Avg: 4.6 receptions / 50.4 receiving yards

Like most of the Panthers offense, DJ Moore has seen a steep regression since his hot start to 2021. After Sam Darnold’s shoulder injury, the team has turned to PJ Walker and brought back Cam Newton to quarterback its offense. While he was able to find the endzone in Week 11 for the first time since Week 4, Moore’s reception and yardage woes are unlikely to improve any time soon without much of a vertical passing threat from this offense.

Russell Wilson

QB | Seahawks

Week 1-3 Avg: 298.3 passing yards

Week 4-11* Avg: 167.3 passing yards *missed three games to injury

Russell Wilson is back from his finger injury, though his first two games have left much to be desired. While his finger may still be bothering him, it also hasn’t helped that head coach Pete Carroll has publicly come out and announced his desire for the team to get back to running the football. If anybody wants to send out Lucra contests based on Wilson’s early season passing numbers, you should jump at the opportunity to take the other side!
