    1.1. Introduction. This section describes the process for handling technical support requests, incident management and incident resolution by Lucra. In the event a defect, bug, or other issue affecting the Customized Lucra Platform is detected by either Lucra or Client, the issue will be categorized as an incident pursuant to the criteria below. Each incident will be communicated to the defined contacts and resolved as defined in the table below. This Service Level Agreement describes the process for issues that affect the Customized Lucra Platform directly, and excludes issues initiated from Lucra’s own third-party integrations and vendor relationships. In the case of issues with Lucra’s third-party vendors or integrations, Lucra will communicate timing and a resolution plan on an issue-by-issue basis, depending on that specific vendor’s Service Level Agreement with Lucra.

    1.2. Priority Level Definitions and Assignments. Each incident will be assigned a priority level based on the criteria listed below.

    1.2.1. Severity Level 1 (“Critical”). A Severity Level 1 problem means any of the following:

    • A complete outage of a critical service
    • An outage that affects 25% or more of the Lucra SDK users
    • A recurring temporary outage of a critical service
    • Inability to provision a service
    • Loss of data
    • Inability to service user technical support requests
    • Very serious failure rendering the services, or any major function of any of the services, inoperable or severely impaired

    1.2.2. Severity Level 2 (“Serious”). A Severity Level 2 problem means any of the following:

    • A significant degradation of the service
    • An outage that affects 10-25% of the Lucra SDK users
    • Results are materially different from those described in the product definition.
    • Serious failure of the services that causes material inconvenience to Client, its Affiliates and/or their users

    1.2.3. Severity Level 3 (“Moderate”). A Severity Level 3 problem means any of the following:

    • A minor degradation of the service
    • An outage that affects 5-10% of the Lucra SDK users
    • Recent modifications to the system cause services to operate in a way that is materially different from those described in the product definition for non-essential features.
    • A failure of the services that causes material inconvenience to Client, its Affiliates, and/or their users

    1.2.4. Severity Level 4 (“Minor”). A Severity Level 4 problem means any of the following:

    • A small system delay, but no loss of data was experienced or a minor application error occurred
    • Services do not operate strictly according to the documentation and/or technical requirements
