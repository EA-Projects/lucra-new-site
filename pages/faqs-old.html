General FAQ’s

  • What devices can I play on?

    Lucra is currently available on iOS supported phones and is coming soon on other devices.

  • How old do I have to be to play?

    You must be at least 18 years old to play.

  • How do I sign up?

    Signing up is easy! Just download the Lucra Sports iOS app on your phone and follow the sign up flow once you open the app.

  • How do I withdraw funds?

    You can withdraw funds at any time by swiping up on the account bar in the home screen, tapping the settings wheel on the top right, and clicking “Cashout Funds.” You can choose whether to receive funds via ACH (direct) deposit or a physical check. Note that bank transfers may take 3-5 business days to complete and checks may take up to 10 business days to be sent. Reach out to support@lucrasports.com with any issues related to withdrawals.

  • What are Lucra Bucks?

    Lucra Bucks are Lucra’s version of “free play.” They are awarded for things like referrals and can be used in place of cash on the platform, however they have no cash value (not eligible for withdrawal) until they are used to create or accept contests. Once they are used, they are treated like cash.

  • How do I deposit funds?

    On the home screen, swipe up on the account bar and click “add funds.” You can also go to the “contests” tab and create or accept a contest and if you don’t have enough funds in your account, you will be prompted to add funds. We accept credit and debit cards.

  • Why are some users asked provide my passport or license information?

    In order to make the platform a safe place for users to play against their friends using real money, we need either passport or license information in order to verify your information. This is a process called “Know Your Customer” or “KYC”. This is an important step for us to keep Lucra safe and fun for all our users.

Games You Play FAQ’S

  • How do I play?

    Victory is sweet, but victory over your friends is just a little sweeter. With Lucra’s new Games You Play feature, staking money against your friends has never been easier. Long gone are the days of waiting for a friend to pay you back, or not remembering who won the last competition. With the Games You Play, all you have to do is matchup with a friend, pick whichever activity you're playing, and tell us who won. Lucra’s got the rest covered. Whether it’s bar games, sports, fitness, board games, or video games, competing with friends has never been easier! Here’s an example of what a contest might look like: if Carly challenges Dylan on a game of ping pong for $10, Carly would send Dylan a contest proposal on Lucra. Dylan would accept this contest before the match begins. If Dylan wins the match, both users would input the match results into Lucra and the winnings would be sent to Dylan automatically. If there is any contest dispute over who won the match, both players enter their own result and are immediately refunded and the contest is voided.

    1. What are Games You Play?
      1. Games You Play involves two users going head to head in a skill based activity with stakes on the line.(e.g. Amy stakes $10 that she will beat Ryan at golf) Games You Play provided by Lucra allows users to stake money in a variety of skill-based activities such as sports, yard games, drinking games, board games, and video games. With Lucra, users no longer have to worry about the reliability of their counterparts when competing for dough because we hold the money in escrow until the competition is completed. Additionally, users will be able to keep track of their individual stats as well as their head to head stats vs friends

        • Bar games

    3. Where do I find Games You Play on the Lucra app?
      • The Games You Play feature can be found in a tab on the bottom screen of the Lucra app. It is labeled “Games You Play” in the bottom right corner of the screen. If you are in a state where Lucra’s live sports contests are illegal, the Games You Play feature will show up as the home screen on the app.
    4. Does Lucra take a percentage of my winnings?
      • No! The Games You Play feature on the Lucra app is completely free to use. Make Lucra your digital scorekeeper so you always know who has bragging rights.
    5. What states are Games You Play active in?
      • Please refer to our terms of service for eligible states for Lucra sports contests.
    6. Can I change the spread of a Games You Play contest?
      • You cannot change the spread of a Games You Play contest. All Games You Play contests have an even spread and are “heads-up” contests.
    7. What happens if me and my friend disagree on the winner of the contest?
      • If you and your friend disagree on the outcome of your contest, you both will need to represent that in the Lucra app. When you do this, Lucra will refund both of you the amount you staked on the contest.
    8. How does Lucra know who won the Games You Play contest?
      • Both users involved will acknowledge who won the contests through Lucra and the victor will receive their winnings once both participants input the results of the contest.
    9. Can these contests be published to the public feed?
      • No! Games You Play contests can only be sent privately amongst friends or in groups.
    10. Do I have to keep score of the game myself?
      • Yes. Lucra requires you to provide information on who won the contest afterwards, so keeping score is very important for users.
    11. What happens to my money if the contest is canceled?
      • Lucra returns all money in its entirety to users if their Games You Play contests are canceled.
    12. Can I use Lucrabucks on Games You Play contests?
      • Yes! Users can wager Lucrabucks as well as deposit funds to compete against friends.
    13. Are Lucra’s Games You Play contests on a separate app?
      • No! Lucra spectator contests are able to be played on the same app as Lucra’s Games You Play contests.
    14. Can multiple people participate in one Games You Play contest?
      • No! For now, Games You Play contests are only 1v1, we are working on some fun features for group play coming soon.

Sports You Watch FAQ’s

  • How do I play?

    Lucra is a new player vs. player game in which you stake real money on a player and stat (e.g., Lebron James points) versus another user’s player and stat (e.g., Steph Curry points) in what we call a “contest.” Players can be from the same or different sports, as long as they both compete during a given week and the stats selected for each player can be the same or different (e.g., can be Tom Brady touchdowns vs. Tiger Woods birdies). Unlike daily fantasy or sports betting apps, you set your own terms (odds and spread) for contests based on publicly available data that we provide in the app, removing the dynamic where you’re betting against the House. Finish up by choosing whether to send contests to a friend, a group, or the Lucra public feed. Contests take less than one minute to create on average, leaving you time to explore the social features that are at the heart of what we built, including friending other users, creating groups, smack talking, and viewing head-to-head stats. To try it out, once you’ve signed up, tap the “create a contest” button or navigate to “contests” to accept a contest that someone else created. Start building out your Lucra network by adding friends or creating public or private groups on the “friends” tab. Finally, swipe up on your account bar at the bottom of the home page to easily track your contests and winnings at any time.

  • What is an in–game contest?

    In–game contesting, or in-game wagering, allows you to make wagers after the game has already started. The structure of the contest is similar to a normal Lucra contest, except that the contest expires 3 minutes after creation

  • Can I create an in–game contest at any point in a game?

    Each sport has specific rules around when during the game you can create an in–game contest. The following amount of time must be left in a game in order to wager on each of the following:

    · Football - at least one quarter

    · Basketball - at least one quarter

    · Hockey - at least one period

    · Soccer - at least a full half

    · Golf - at least nine (9) holes

    · Tennis - at least one set

    · Baseball - at least two innings

  • Can I post an in–game contest to the public feed?

    Lucra does not currently support sending in–game contests to the public feed. Send it to your friend on Lucra instead!

  • Where can I play?

    Please refer to our terms of service for eligible states for Lucra sports contests.

  • What happens if a contest hasn’t been accepted before the start of a competition
    that one of the players is playing in?

    If a contest isn’t accepted prior to the start of the competition, then the contest will be voided and the full amount staked will be returned to the user’s available funds.

  • What sports do you offer?

    We currently offer NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, golf, tennis, soccer (MLS, UEFA Champions League, Premier League), and NASCAR, and are always looking to add more sports. Shoot us an email at support@lucrasports.com if there’s a sport or competition that you’d like to see in Lucra in the future and we’ll work on adding it.

  • When do contests close?

    You can create or accept contests that involve any two players from the same or different sports as long as they both have competitions that week that have not yet begun. Once a competition starts, you will be unable to create a contest involving a player from that game.

  • What happens if a player doesn’t play?

    If an athlete doesn’t play (for injury, illness, or any other reason) prior to the start of the competition, then a contest involving that athlete will be canceled and all wagers will be refunded.

  • Can I edit a contest once it has been created?

    Once you create a contest, you may edit it as long as nobody has accepted the other side. As soon as an opponent accepts the contest, it is live and can no longer be edited.
